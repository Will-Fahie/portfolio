Courses and MOOCs

"Everything was impossible until someone did it."

- Scott Dinsmore



Anyone who knows me will agree: I love nothing more than learning new things. I think online courses or "MOOCs" are a fantastic for two main reasons:

1. The most obvious one: you get to learn new stuff! There are so many courses out there that no matter what area you might find interesting, you can probably learn about it.

2. Online courses serve as an amazing way to get a feel for a certain topic. This helps with searching for that particular field/space that you find captivating. In this way, courses have helped me to get a better idea of where I want to go in the future.

Understanding Einstein: The Special Theory of Relativity

This was a fantastic course I did over the summer of 2020. I learned about Einstein's life and his Special Theory of Relativity, taking about 80 hours in total. You can view it by clicking here!

Here's the course outline:

1. Einstein in context
2. Events, clocks and reference frames
3. Etheral problems and solutions
4. The weirdness begins
5. Spacetime switches
6. Breaking the spacetime speed limit
7. Paradoxes to ponder
8. To the centre of the galaxy and back



Cosmic Rays, Dark Matter, and the Mysteries of the Universe

I completed this course in October 2020. It was a highly mathematical course, and was aimed at post A-level students, thus I felt a little out of my depth! However, I did learn a few interesting things. You can check it out here!

The syllabus included:

1. The evolution of the universe
2. The origin and propagation of cosmic rays
3. The search for dark matter through cosmic ray observation

ZeroToMastery: Complete Python Developer in 2021

This was a brilliant course that covered the Python language extensively - from the basics to some pretty advanced concepts. After learning the language and syntax (which I already had a good knowledge of), I got to complete some fun projects including web scraping, scripting, automation, back-end web development and more. View it here.

ZeroToMastery: Machine learning and Data Science Bootcamp

I enjoyed my first course from the people at ZeroToMastery (most notably, Andrei Neagoie) so much that I just had to do another! They offer a wide range of programming courses, but I had this burning desire to learn about machine learning and data science. This area of programming allows me to combine skills from computer science and physics (my two biggest areas of interest) in certain projects. Furthermore, Python is the most widely used language for it. It was the obvious choice! Click here to have a look.
