Extended Project Qualification

"Let our advance worrying become advance thinking and planning."

- Winston Churchill



An extended project qualification, usually just called the "EPQ", is something one can take on alongside their A levels. It is equivalent to just over half an A level, in that it's the length of an AS level, but you can achieve an A* (AS levels only go up to a grade A). It is essentially a qualification in project management: you decide a question, create a plan, research the topic and create a report to answer the question. I just to do mine on something that has always fascinated me... the colonisation of Mars!


Report

I originally wanted to cover a huge amount, however, an EPQ report has a limit of 5,000 words. Thus, I had to compress it down to something more feasible. My final report title was "Building a self-sufficient colony on Mars: an evaluation of the options." The report covered four main things. One was a "prerequisite": general resource acquisition approach (GRAA), which explores the attitude to actually acquiring the resources on Mars (i.e. asteroid mining or in-situ resource utilisation). The four main ones were that of producing oxygen, water and food - the three basic survival requirements of humans. There are obviously other aspects that are required for humans to live on Mars (such as clothing and shelter), but one may consider these three "the most vital". Therefore, my report was really an exploration of the fundamental feasability of living self-sufficiently on Mars with technology that we possess today. You can view the report by clicking here.
